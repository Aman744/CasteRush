The Caste Rush

The 60-minute documentary seeks to shed light on the realities that mainstream narratives often overlook. Traveling across India, it captures the presence of priests from various sections of Hindu society including Dalits within Hindu temple ecosystems—an aspect rarely highlighted. It also showcases the non-retributive, reconciliatory, and reformist efforts of individuals who have uplifted marginalized communities.


Additionally, the documentary captures how, at a mass level, caste discrimination is not observed in Hindu temple rituals and gatherings, countering many prevailing misconceptions. It also examines how the current caste discourse in the West affects the Indian and Hindu diaspora, shaping public perceptions and policies. 


The goal is to dispel misconceptions and stereotypes that adversely impact young minds, policymakers, and misinformed parents. Much like the Gold Rush of the 19th century, where people were drawn toward sudden wealth, a new kind of "rush" has emerged—this time, around caste. However, unlike the Gold Rush, which fueled economic prosperity and growth, the Caste Rush is leaving behind division, hostility, and misinformation, harming the very communities it claims to uplift. In the 1850s, America witnessed a frenzied pursuit of fortune following the discovery of gold in California. This Gold Rush triggered a massive influx of people from across the United States, leaving a lasting impact by driving economic growth and shaping California’s future in mostly positive ways. In contrast, the Caste Rush has unfolded in multiple phases, each with damaging consequences. The first major event was the California lawsuit against Cisco and its employees over alleged caste discrimination. Though the case was eventually dismissed—demonstrating that the accused were unfairly targeted because of their Indian heritage and caste—the damage had already been done. The lawsuit fueled a wave of anti-Hindu sentiment and hostility, deepening misunderstandings and fears across the country. Next came California State Bill SB-403, which sought to amend the state’s civil, administrative, and government codes to include caste as a protected category. However, after a long and determined campaign by individuals, organizations, and institutions, the Governor of California ultimately vetoed the bill.


Although these battles have been legally won, the Caste Rush has left lasting scars on the Hindu diaspora in the West. The persistent misrepresentation of caste has fueled anti-Hindu hate, leading to increasing attacks on Hindu temples, and the shaming of Hindu religious, cultural, and nonprofit organizations. This distorted narrative has a subtle but profound effect—especially on younger generations—alienating them from their cultural roots and ancestry. The weaponization of caste in political discourse—both in India and abroad—has only deepened divisions and mistrust, rather than fostering real progress. Despite these challenges, many organizations, community leaders, student activists, and politicians from both sides have stood up to protect the unity and safety of the Indian and Hindu diaspora. The documentary The Caste Rush aims to reveal the true impact of this rush and challenge the one-sided narratives that dominate public discourse. By presenting on-the-ground realities from India and exposing the damaging effects of caste discourse in the West, it seeks to provide a more balanced and factual perspective. Unlike the Gold Rush, which shaped California’s future for the better, the Caste Rush threatens to divide communities and sow long-term discord. The question remains—will society recognize the harm being done, or will this manufactured rush continue to deepen divides?
