The Caste Rush

About the Film

The 60-minute documentary seeks to shed light on the realities that mainstream narratives often overlook. Traveling across India, it captures the presence of priests from various sections of Hindu society including Dalits within Hindu temple ecosystems—an aspect rarely highlighted. It also showcases the non-retributive, reconciliatory, and reformist efforts of individuals who have uplifted marginalized communities. Additionally, the documentary captures how, at a mass level, caste discrimination is not observed in Hindu temple rituals and gatherings, countering many prevailing misconceptions. It also examines how the current caste discourse in the West affects the Indian and Hindu diaspora, shaping public perceptions and policies.
