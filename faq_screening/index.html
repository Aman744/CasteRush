Brief description or purpose of event (cast/crew, employee or client appreciation, educational, fundraiser, etc.):

This is an educational documentary subject related to Indian American community. The director of the documentary is doing USA tour, he will be present at the theater and interact with audience. The name of the director is Nikhil Singh.

About Indic Dialogue

Indic Dialogue aims to spark informed conversations and build awareness around issues affecting India and Hindus globally. As part of this mission, we bring attention to voices that are often unheard—especially those affected by human rights violations.

Indic Dialogue has arranged talks on various topics across the United States by personalities such as Ram Madhav, Rajiv Malhotra, Swami Vignanand ji, and Prof Walter Anderson among many other esteemed names.

I would like to take a moment to thank the team who put this film together.

Nikhil Singh Rajputt is the Director of “CasteRush”

Nikhil Singh Rajputt was born in Chhapra-Bihar and completed his High School in Varanasi and his Secondary School in Ranchi. He prepared for his engineering from Kota-Rajasthan and graduated from IIT in Delhi with a B.Tech in Textile Technology {2007}. He was an Assistant Director in the Mumbai Film Industry and later attended a Summer Program at the University Of Southern California [USC] Film School {2009}. His debut film was an Indie Bollywood feature 'With Love, Delhi! [2011].” He is also known for his award-winning documentaries, namely Himalayan Refugee [2017], Blood Buddhas [2019] and Stateless [2021]. “Stateless” - a documentary many of you have also seen which focuses on the state of Hindu refugees from Pakistan in India was produced by Indic Dialogue in 2021.

Talking points

What Inspired the Movie Caste Rush:

  • Cisco caste discrimination lawsuit
  • California SB 403 bill – proposed adding caste as a protected category like age, sex, and religion; later vetoed.
  • 2021 Dismantling Global Hindutva Conference – equated Hinduism with casteism and called for its dismantling.
  • Ongoing debates linking caste to Hinduism in academia and public discourse.
  • School textbooks teach caste discrimination in temples as historical fact, raising questions like:
  • Are Dalits barred from temples?
  • Can non-Brahmins be priests?
  • Is caste-based discrimination still present in Hindu temples in India?

Why We Should Watch This Film

Talk this right before screening inside the cinema hall to audiences.
The dangers of targeting Hindus and equating Hinduism with casteism, like in CISCO case or SB403 bill, is not over yet. These things have a way to be resurrected by interested adversaries in future. We need to be prepared

Educate Non-Hindus:
The Caste Rush aims to inform non-Hindus, especially educators, about Hinduism’s complexities, countering leftist misinformation.

Clarify Context:
Bridges knowledge gaps for teachers and policymakers unfamiliar with Hinduism’s inclusive traditions.

Provide Evidence:
Uses scholar and community interviews as credible talking points for educating non-Hindus.

Accessible Tool:
60-minute runtime makes it easy to share with non-Hindu educators and administrators.

Correct Stereotypes:
Helps challenge misrepresentations and promote accurate Hinduism portrayals in schools.

Parent/Citizen Action:
As Hindu Americans we need to recommend the documentary to schools and discuss accurate curricula with educators.

Student Action:
Watch with parents to confidently explain Hindu values and correct misconceptions in academic settings.
Lets watch the movie with this perspective and look for and note down the credible talking points for engaging with the outside world to take on the misconceptions. Let's think of ways and places that we can seek to have this documentary watched, shared in the years to come.

About the Documentary:

  • A 60-minute truth-seeking journey across India to examine caste discrimination in ancient temples.
  • Covers 6 states and 12+ temples, some over 1500 years old.
  • Uses testimonies from ordinary people in temple ecosystems.
  • Reveals Jaatis (communities) coexisting in harmony within Hindu society.
  • Highlights self-driven societal reforms where discrimination exists.

Core Message:

Promote harmony among Jaatis.
Share this message in schools, colleges, workplaces, and with officials that Hinduism is inclusive and promotes harmony, respect and interdependence amongst different jatis. Do study,understand Varna/Jati – their original purpose and how colonial powers exploited them
